Customer Service

Customer service that actually sells

By Ruth Jackson· 7 min read· From the banking hall

I spent years in banking customer service before I became a coach. People assume the job is about being polite. It isn't, it's about being trusted. And trust, it turns out, is the cheapest and most powerful marketing a business can have.

Every complaint is a second chance

A customer who complains is still talking to you. The ones who leave quietly are gone forever. Handled well, a complaint becomes the moment a customer decides you're worth staying with.

The goal isn't to "win" the conversation. It's to make the person feel heard, then solved.

The 3-step recovery that works every time

  • Acknowledge, name the feeling before the facts. "I can hear this has been frustrating."
  • Own it, even if it isn't your fault, own the resolution. "Let me sort this for you."
  • Close the loop, follow up. The follow-up is what they remember.

Consistency beats charisma

One brilliant employee can't carry a brand. A simple, shared standard, how you greet, how you respond, how you follow up, makes every customer's experience reliable. Reliability is what gets recommended.

Service is a growth strategy, not a cost

Happy customers come back, spend more, and bring their friends. In markets where everyone sells similar things at similar prices, how you treat people is often the only real difference. That difference compounds.

Make it your team's culture

This is exactly what I help businesses build, not a one-off pep talk, but standards and habits the whole team can own. I tailor every session to your customers and your industry.
